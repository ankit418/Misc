Abstract:

A walker or walking frame is a tool for disabled or elderly people who need additional support to maintain balance or stability while walking. While such standard walkers provide a stable Support when used on level Surfaces. However, they are highly unstable and unsafe when used on Stairs and inclined surfaces. A surface-levelling reconfigurable walker with stair and ramp climbing ability was designed and developed for those people requiring walkers to travel on either level ground or inclined ground, and to negotiate both the ascent and descent of Stairs. There are two racks (one connected to frame and one to movable leg) in both legs (4 racks in total), legs can be engaged and disengaged using a hand lever. It provides highly precise locking positions while.using walker on stairs/ramps. This uniquely designed irregular surface levelling walker aid promises to eliminate the requirement for multiple aids, assistance on Stairs, inclined surfaces. Moreover, it also eliminates limitation that existed both socially and physically within the environment. This outcome therefore helps and support the market to increase use of personal mobility through integrating new and feasible mechanism in addition to redesigning and manufacturing the conventional walker.
